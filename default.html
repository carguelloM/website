About Me

I am a PhD student at Dartmouth College working with David Kotz and Tim Pierson. I am currently working on the SPLICE project. More specifically, I am working on the development of Harmonic Radar Technology (Non-Linear Juction Detectors) for the detection of consumer electronics.

I am interested in the various aspects of cyber security, including cryptosystems, physical exploits (e.g. side-channel attacks), and IoT security. My default editor is Vim! (I secretly use VS Code). I was born in Managua, Nicaragua. My hobbies include traveling, reading, and watching Formula 1. My birthday is on Star Wars Day!
